Company

At the end of the 19th century, no one could dream of consoles and video games, so the history of of Nintendo began with the production of cards for the popular board game Hanafuda. Fusajiro Yamauchi and his team were among the leaders in their niche, and the sales of the cards were generating a steady income. However, the business needed to scale, to grow out of humble beginnings. As times passed, the second president of Nintendo, Sekiry≈ç Yamauchi, grew old. His grandson, Hiroshi Yamauchi, had to take over the business. In 1963 a rebranding took place, which gave the company a new name, Nintendo Co, which began to develop handheld and stationary consoles. Such iconic characters as Mario, Donkey Kong, etc. emerged from under their wings. Nintendo continues to evolve in new directions to this day, not limited to video games and consoles. The organization is engaged in the development and production of interactive simulators for military personnel. They allow to improve the quality of shooting. The prototype for this simulator was the game "Duck Hunt".
