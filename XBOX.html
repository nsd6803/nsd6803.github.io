Company

It all started when four guys from the DirectX development team got together in Fantastic Four and started nurturing the idea of creating their own game console. Seamus Blackley, Kevin Bachus, Otto Berkes, Ted Hayes, wanted to make features like the PC, but with a simple UI and fast loading games like the consoles, support for DirectX, and be far enough ahead of Sony in performance. In 1999, Project Four reached Gates, he said "good guys," approved an early technical plan, and put Bacchus and Rick Thompson to build a business model. The working name was DirectX Box (after the main project), but was shortened to Xbox. The team began interviewing developers, finding out what they wanted in a console so they wouldn't have to steamroll at all. Meanwhile, engineers were designing new versions of the console, and Gates was sending them back, saying it wasn't powerful enough for people to see the difference between the PS2 and their brainchild. Engineers decided to settle on a bunch of Intel Nvidia, so the Xbox itself, built on x86 architecture, resembled a computer in terms of hardware than a console.
